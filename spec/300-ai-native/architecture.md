# Abstract

Monad is designed as an AI-native engineering platform.

AI is not an external assistant layered on top of the system.

AI is an integrated participant operating within the Engineering Model and Control Plane.

The AI architecture enables intelligent agents to understand, reason about, and safely modify engineering systems.

---

# 1. AI Native Definition

An AI-native system is one where AI capabilities are foundational to system operation.

AI is integrated into:

- information representation
- decision-making
- automation
- optimization
- interaction

---

# 2. AI Architecture Principles

## Context First

AI reasoning MUST be grounded in the Engineering Model.

AI systems SHOULD reason from:

- objects
- relationships
- policies
- history
- evidence

---

## Governed Intelligence

AI actions MUST operate within:

- identity
- permissions
- policies
- audit requirements

---

## Human Partnership

AI augments human engineering capability.

Humans remain accountable for:

- intent
- critical decisions
- organizational goals

---

## Continuous Learning

AI systems SHOULD improve through:

- historical outcomes
- feedback
- evaluations
- system knowledge

Monad AI architecture consists of:

Generates conclusions and plans.

---

---

## Memory Layer

Preserves knowledge over time.

---

## Evaluation Layer

Measures performance and reliability.

- architecture
- ownership
- dependencies
- policies

# 7. AI Actions

AI-generated actions are equivalent to actions from other actors.

They require:

- identity
- authorization
- provenance
- audit history

---

# 8. AI Safety Model

Monad AI systems MUST support:

- bounded autonomy
- explainability
- rollback
- approval workflows
- policy enforcement

---

# 9. Multiple AI Systems

Monad SHOULD support multiple AI providers and models.

The architecture MUST avoid dependency on a single model provider.

---

# 10. AI Model Independence

AI models are replaceable components.

The durable assets are:

- Engineering Model
- Context
- Policies
- Knowledge
- Workflows

---

# 11. AI and Automation

AI agents may:

- identify problems
- generate plans
- propose changes
- execute approved workflows

Automation provides execution guarantees.

AI provides reasoning capability.

---

# 12. AI Evaluation

AI capabilities SHOULD be measured.

Evaluation SHOULD consider:

- accuracy
- safety
- reliability
- usefulness
- cost

---

# 13. AI Architecture Principle

Monad AI Native Architecture defines:

> A governed intelligence layer where AI agents understand engineering systems through semantic models and safely participate in their evolution.
